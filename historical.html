Historical Federal Investment Returns

Evidence that when the U.S. government makes major investments in strategic sectors, private investors who position themselves early have historically been rewarded.

1958–1970s

NASA & The Space Race

Government Investment

  • • NASA budget peaked at ~4% of the entire federal budget in the mid-1960s
  • • Massive contracts for Apollo program, satellites, and research
  • • Created entire new industries in aerospace, computing, and materials science

Companies That Benefited

Boeing
Major Apollo contractor → became global aerospace leader
Lockheed Martin
Key defense & space contractor, long-term growth
IBM
Built computers for Apollo → dominated enterprise computing
Northrop Grumman
Grew significantly through government space contracts
Key Lesson: Early positioning in government-backed high-tech sectors led to multi-decade growth for contractors and suppliers. Many of these companies became foundational to the U.S. economy.
1960s–1990s

ARPANET & The Internet

Government Investment

  • • DARPA (then ARPA) funded the creation of ARPANET in 1969
  • • Decades of government R&D spending on networking, computing, and communications
  • • Eventually led to the commercial internet boom of the 1990s

Companies That Benefited

Cisco Systems
Built routers that powered the early internet → massive growth
Intel
Semiconductors powered by government research demand
Microsoft & Apple
Indirect beneficiaries as computing became mainstream
Amazon, Google, Meta
Later built on the infrastructure created by government investment
Key Lesson: Foundational government research spending created the conditions for trillions in private sector value creation decades later.
1925–1980s

Bell Labs & Fundamental Research

Government Investment

  • • Massive military and government contracts during WWII and Cold War
  • • AT&T (parent company) operated as a regulated monopoly with government support
  • • Funded groundbreaking basic research that private markets alone might not have supported

Major Breakthroughs & Impact

Transistor (1947)
Foundation of all modern electronics and computing
Laser & Solar Cell
Enabled entire new industries in optics and energy
Information Theory
Claude Shannon’s work underpins all digital communication
Unix & C Language
Basis for modern operating systems and software
Key Lesson: Government-supported basic research at Bell Labs created foundational technologies that powered trillions in economic value across multiple industries for decades.
Post-WWII to Present

Universities & Federal Research Funding

Government Investment

  • • Post-WWII surge in federal funding for university research (NSF, NIH, DARPA, DOE)
  • • Billions annually in grants supporting basic and applied science
  • • Created the modern U.S. research university system

Major Impacts & Companies Spawned

Stanford University
Federal research funding helped birth Silicon Valley (HP, Google, etc.)
MIT
Defense and computing research led to companies like Raytheon, Draper Labs spinouts
Biotech Revolution
NIH funding led to Genentech, Amgen, Moderna and modern pharmaceuticals
GPS & Computing
DARPA and university research enabled GPS, internet protocols, and AI foundations
Key Lesson: Sustained federal investment in university research has been one of the highest-ROI public investments in history, creating entire new industries and trillions in economic value.
These historical examples show a consistent pattern: when the federal government makes large, sustained investments in strategic technologies and research, companies and institutions positioned to support or commercialize those investments have often delivered extraordinary long-term returns for investors and society.
